Let’s go Bananas: Top 5 reasons to indulge in this fruit

One fruit that has been intriguing modern generation for its variety of benefits is definitely Banana. Here are top five reasons to make this fruit a part of your regular diet.

A fruit or a meal

The current generation which is always on the run wants to use its time efficiently and here comes banana to the rescue by providing a healthy snacking option or even replacing a meal. A medium banana is 105 calories with 25 g of carbs and 3 g of fiber. It is also rich in potassium, vitamin C, vitamin A, magnesium and vitamin B6. This makes bananas a great mid-afternoon snack or a perfect pre-workout snack if you eat them 1 hour before your workout.

Eat banana and lose weight

Bananas contain resistant starch, a type of carbohydrate you can’t digest, but helps you feel full for longer period of time. This creates a pseudo-feeling of hunger satisfaction and keeps you away from unnecessary munching. Bananas are low in calories and have no fat, no sodium, and no cholesterol. Additionally Vitamin B6 in banana can also help protect against type 2 diabetes and also aid in weight loss.

You need a heart to love and banana keeps it healthy

A medium banana has 420 mg potassium and no sodium. The high potassium-to-sodium ratio helps to prevent the risk of high blood pressure which can be caused by high sodium in your diet. Various studies show that potassium-rich diet can help lower blood pressure, and people who eat plenty of potassium have up to a 27% lower risk of heart disease. A medium-sized banana provides 10 percent of the daily requirement of potassium in your diet.

Banana keeps you happy

Bananas can be helpful in overcoming depression due to high levels of tryptophan, which the body converts to serotonin and melatonin, two important mood and sleep-regulating neurotransmitters. Plus, vitamin B6 can help you sleep well, and magnesium helps to relax muscles. Bananas are the only fruit that contains both tryptophan and vitamin B6. Banana seems to be a super fruit that can relieve you from all mood swings and trivial worries of the world.

Food of the athletes

Bananas are more effective in replenishing energy and electrolytes than an average sports drink according a study conducted by PLOS One. Researchers conclude that athletic performance is same with having banana and water instead of sports drink but banana additionally provides serotonin and dopamine which improves the athletes' antioxidant capacity and helped with oxidative stress, giving better overall result.



The scientific name for banana is musa sapientum, which means “fruit of the wise men.” May be it is wise to include banana in your diet.
